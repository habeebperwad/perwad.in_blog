
				
					Lu Guanqiu got his start in business by acquiring scrap metal and turning it into farm tools during China's Cultural Revolution.
				
				
					Today, his Wanxiang Group is one of China's largest conglomerates with more than 40,000 employees.
				
				
					Its interests are broad, including real estate, agribusiness and finance, but its core is auto parts. 
				
				
					Heir apparent is son Lu Weiding, whose personal personal investments include film maker Huayi Brothers Media.
				
				
					 Wanxiang America Corporation, based in Chicago, is run by Lu's son-in-law Ni Pin.
