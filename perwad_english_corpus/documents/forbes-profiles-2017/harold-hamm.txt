
			The fracking tycoon founded his first company, Harold Hamm Tank Trucks, in 1966. Today his fortune swings with the share price of Continental Resources. In 2014, to settle his divorce Hamm wrote his wife a check for $975 million.  The 13th child of Oklahoma sharecroppers, Hamm grew up poor. As a teenager, he pumped gas and changed tires. Continental produces more than 200,000 barrels per day, most of it from North Dakota's Bakken formation.
