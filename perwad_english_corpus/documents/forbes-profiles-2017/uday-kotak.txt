
			After acquiring rival ING Vysya Bank in 2014, Kotak Mahindra Bank, controlled by Uday Kotak, is reaping the benefits of that merger. Net profit in a recent quarter was up close to 40% to $130 million. Kotak, who is required by India's central bank to bring down his one-third holding in the bank to 30% by June, recently sold a 1.5% stake for $337 million to two Canadian institutional investors.  He ruled out a rumour that his bank was mulling a merger with rival Axis Bank. Spurning his family's trading business Kotak started a finance firm in 1985 then went on convert it into a bank in 2003.
