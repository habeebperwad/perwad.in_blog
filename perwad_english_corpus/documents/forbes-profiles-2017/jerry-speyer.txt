
			Property developer Jerry Speyer, through his firm Tishman Speyer, has owned, redeveloped or managed 152 million square feet including landmarks like Rockefeller Center and the Chrysler Building in Manhattan and skyscrapers in Chicago, Berlin, London, Frankfurt and Paris.  Jerry is the chairman and his son Rob is CEO. (Until 2015, they held co-CEO titles). The firm also has developments in China. Its flagship project there is The Springs, a 66-acre mixed-use complex outside of Shanghai, where Nike is one of the office tenants. The firm also has real estate equity funds with more than $12 billion in net assets under management. Speyer started the firm with his late father-in-law, Robert Tishman, in 1978. The marriage ended in divorce, but the company survived. Speyer is also chairman of the Museum of Modern Art and vice chairman of New York Presbyterian Hospital. His wife Katherine chairs the Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts and oversees Tishman Speyer's China and Brazil business.
