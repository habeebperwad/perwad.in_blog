
			Octogenarian investment manager Fayez Sarofim still doubles as the chairman and CEO of his money management firm, Fayez Sarofim & Co. Sarofim, nicknamed "The Sphinx," was one of the first investors in energy pipeline firm Kinder Morgan.  He stepped down as a director at the time of the firm's IPO in 2011. The son of a wealthy Egyptian cotton farmer, he received an M.B.A. from Harvard Business School before starting his own investment firm in 1958. He made his fortune as a buy-and-hold specialist with long-term investments in Philip Morris, Coca-Cola, and Procter & Gamble. He sits on the board of the Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center as well as the Texas Heart Institute.
