
			Juan Abello is a Spanish businessman and onetime pharmacist who has built his fortune on a range of investments since selling his family's pharmaceutical company in 1985. He owns a stake in for-profit education company Laureate Education, which paid former U. S. president Bill Clinton $16 million to act as a consultant from 2010 to 2014. Abello's other investments include Prestige Motor Holdings, the largest shareholder of the luxurious Aston Martin automobiles and Grupo Imagina, a media holding company. He often appears in tabloids alongside well-known politicians and members of Spanish artistic and cultural circles. Abello is also known for his valuable art collection, which includes works by Pablo Picasso, Francisco Goya, El Greco, Edgar Degas, Toulouse-Lautrec, Salvador Dali, Francis Bacon, and Van Gogh. He's an enthusiastic hunter who enjoys bullfights and soccer.
