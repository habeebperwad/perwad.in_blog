
			Analjit Singh is the youngest son of Bhai Mohan Singh, founder of pharma firm Ranbaxy Laboratories. Following a family split in the 1980s, he inherited a small company called Max India and built it into the $2 billion (revenue) Max Group, which has interests in healthcare, insurance and specialty packaging film.  The Boston University grad, made an early investment in India's telecom sector with cellular services provider Hutchison Max and made a pile when he sold his stake in that business to Hutchison. In 2016, he split Max into three listed companies and went on to merge his insurance arm Max Life with HDFC Life to create the largest private insurer in the country. In recent years, he has invested in South Africa where he owns a vineyard and two boutique resorts. His daughter founded Antara, a senior living community in North India.
