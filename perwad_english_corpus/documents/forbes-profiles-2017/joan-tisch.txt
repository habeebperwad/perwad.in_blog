
			Joan Tisch's husband Bob bought 50% of the New York Giants for $75 million in 1991, and she inherited a stake in the team when he died 14 years later. The Giants are now worth $3.1 billion. Tisch also inherited part of in a diversified company named Loews, which has interests in oil drilling, natural gas pipelines and insurance.  She remains the company's largest individual shareholder. A committed supporter of efforts to combat AIDS and a philanthropist, Tisch funded construction of the Tisch Building at Gay Men's Health Crisis in Manhattan.
