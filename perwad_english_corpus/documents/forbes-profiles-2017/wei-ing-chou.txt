
				
					Wei Ing-Chou and his three brothers,  Ying-Chiao, Yin-Chun and Yin-Heng, founded Cayman Islands-based Tingyi.
				
				
					Tingyi is now one of the largest suppliers of ready-to-drink tea and bottled water in China; it also supplies half of the country's instant noodle market.
				
				
					Ing-Chou is Tingyi's chairman; son Wei Hong-Ming, a member of Tingyi's board, is heir apparent.
				
				
					The family has investments in Taipei 101, Taiwan's tallest building, and the Dicos fried chicken chain.
