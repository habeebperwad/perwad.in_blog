
			Co-founder of King Street Capital Management, a distressed-debt fund with $19 billion in assets that racked up a solid performance through the financial crisis and rebounded in 2016 with a roughly 4.5% return net of fees in its main fund.  Started King Street in 1995 with O. Francis Biondi. The duo met on First Boston's high-yield desk in the late 1980s. Publicity-shy fund manager warned investors that the credit cycle has run its course and could get dicey.
