
			David Sanford "Sandy" Gottesman was an early investor in Berkshire Hathaway, where he now holds the bulk of his fortune. The Harvard Business School grad met Warren Buffett in 1962 and founded his own investment firm, First Manhattan Co. , in 1964. His son, Robert, now runs the company, which has more than $16 billion in assets under management. A Jewish American, Gottesman is a chairman emeritus at Yeshiva University in New York. In 2008, he and his wife Ruth donated $25 million to the school to establish the Ruth L. and David S. Gottesman Institute for Stem Cell Biology and Regenerative Medicine. Gottesman has been a Berkshire board member since 2004 and is on the board for the American Museum of Natural History.
