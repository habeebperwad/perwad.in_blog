After cashing out of Wilmar 7 years ago, former stockbroker Peter Lim has focused on property, healthcare and sports. His Singapore-listed Rowsley is building a $1.2 billion health care city in Malaysia's Iskandar region, targeting the ageing population of Singapore and Malaysia. Rowsley has also expanded in hospitality with a joint venture with former Manchester United football stars Gary Neville and Ryan Giggs to redevelop the Manchester's historic Northern Stock Exchange into a boutique hotel. and another mixed-use development in the city center. The trio is planning to open a string of football-themed hotels and cafes across Asia and Europe over the next decade. Fans of Lim's English football club Valencia, disappointed over the team's recent defeats, held a protest in January calling for the billionaire sell the club.
