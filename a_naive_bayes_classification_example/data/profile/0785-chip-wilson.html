Chip Wilson, the founder and former CEO of Lululemon, opened Lululemon's first store in Vancouver in 2000. He took the firm public in 2007, but stepped down as the chairman in late 2013 and stepped away from the business completely in 2015 after a series of controversies. In 2013, Wilson's blaming Lululemon's too sheer pants on women's body types caused an uproar among fans. Although Wilson has no management role in Lululemon, he remains its biggest individual shareholder. He advises Kit and Ace, a creation of his wife and son that sells washable cashmere-like clothing.
