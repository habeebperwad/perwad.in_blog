Bruce Karsh founded Los Angeles-based investment firm Oaktree Capital Management with longtime business partner Howard Marks in 1995. He still runs the company today as its co-chairman. The firm, which went public in 2012, is leading debt investor, with over half of its $100 billion assets under management invested in corporate or distressed debt. A Duke graduate, Karsh helps fund scholarships there for students from such countries as Ethiopia, Romania and Pakistan. He is also a minority owner of the Golden State Warriors, and made a bid for the Los Angeles Clippers in 2014. Karsh, a Beatles fan, owns the rights to the iconic British band's movie "A Hard Day's Night," and co-owns the rights to "Help!"
