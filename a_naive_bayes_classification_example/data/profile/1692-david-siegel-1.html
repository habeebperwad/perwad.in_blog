The co-founder of Two Sigma Investments, David Siegel has built a quantitative trading powerhouse that is the fastest growing hedge fund in the business. With $40 billion of assets under management, Two Sigma is a data-driven hedge fund that absorbs large amounts of information to predict the prices of securities ranging from stocks to futures contracts. It flagship Spectrum fund returned about 3% in 2016 while its big Compass fund returned about 10% net of fees. Born in the Bronx, Siegel is a computer fanatic who received a computer science PhD from MIT and worked at hedge funds D.E. Shaw and Tudor Investment Corp. He also founded his own Internet start-up before starting Two Sigma with John Ovedeck. The firm also runs a market-making business, owns a Bermuda reinsurance company and operates a venture capital arm.
