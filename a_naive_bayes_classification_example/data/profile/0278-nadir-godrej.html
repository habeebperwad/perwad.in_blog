Nadir Godrej is the younger sibling of billionaire Adi Godrej and holds a 20% share in the assets of the Godrej Group, the 120 year-old consumer goods conglomerate. With degrees from MIT, Stanford University and an MBA from Harvard Business School, he joined the family business more than four decades ago. Today he oversees Godrej Industries as managing director and chairs agribusiness unit Godrej Agrovet. He has a flair for languages and poetry and often delivers public speeches in verse form. As a non-executive director of the Tata group's Indian Hotels, he recently voted in favour of its ousted chairman Cyrus Mistry.
