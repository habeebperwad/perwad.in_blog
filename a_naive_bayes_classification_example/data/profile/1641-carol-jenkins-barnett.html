Carol Jenkins Barnett is an heir to the Publix Super Markets fortune. The daughter of Publix founder George Jenkins, she owns just under 5% of the business, which is the largest employee-owned company in the U. S. Barnett spent 33 years on the Publix board, serving from 1983 until June 2016, when she stepped down after being diagnosed with early onset Alzheimer's disease. She still leads the company's charitable arm, which donates $25 million annually to support education and the homeless. In June 2016 it also donated $250,000 to the OneOrlando fund in the wake of the mass shooting at a nightclub.
