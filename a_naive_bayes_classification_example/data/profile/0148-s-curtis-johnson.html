S. Curtis Johnson is the great-great grandson of Samuel Curtis Johnson, who founded SC Johnson, the family's cleaning products company, in 1886. After pleading guilty to misdemeanor sexual assault of his stepdaughter in June 2014, he was sentenced to four months in jail and fined $6,000. He had been the chairman of family-owned industrial cleaning company Diversey. He stepped down in 2011, citing personal reasons, before his arrest. The company was sold to Sealed Air, the maker of Bubble Wrap, in October 2011 for $4.3 billion. Curt's brother, H. Fisk Johnson, is the current chairman and CEO of SC Johnson. Sister Helen runs Johnson Bank and Johnson Outdoors. His other sister, Winifred, heads up the Johnson Family Foundation.
