Roberto Angelini Rossi heads one of Chile's largest investment groups, Inversiones Angelini. The group's biggest holding is publicly traded Antarchile, which distributes fuel, operates fishing and shipping businesses and invests in energy, forestry and mining. Roberto Angelini also owns stakes in agribusiness firm Siemel and commercial fishing outfit Eperva. His sister Patricia Angelini Rossi also owns a stake in the family group. The Angelini family is very private, typically eschewing publicity. The group was previously headed by Roberto's uncle, Anacleto Angelini, who died in 2007.
