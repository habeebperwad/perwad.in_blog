J. Christopher Flowers runs private equity firm J.C. Flowers & Co., which focuses on the financial services sector. He studied math at Harvard and then joined Goldman Sachs in 1979, becoming a partner in 1988 before striking out on his own a decade later. In 2000 J.C. Flowers bought a beleaguered Japanese bank, the Long Term Credit Bank, which had been nationalized in 1998. He resold the business to the public under the moniker Shinsei Bank in 2004; partners reaped $2.3 billion. In 2008, he helped advise Bank of America and Merrill Lynch on their merger.
