H. Fisk Johnson is the fifth generation of the Johnson family to lead SC Johnson, the privately-held cleaning products company. H. Fisk became chairman of the $10 billion (sales) company in 2000 and took over as CEO when his father Samuel Curtis (d. 2004) stepped down. Today SC Johnson is best known for products such as Windex, Off! and Fantastik. His sister Helen runs Johnson Bank and publicly traded Johnson Outdoors. Another sister, Winifred, runs the Johnson Family Foundation. His mother Imogene Powers Johnson is the founding director and chairman of the Prairie School, a college prep day school originally built for the children of SC Johnson factory workers. In 2017, Fisk and SC Johnson pledged $150 million to Cornell University's College of Business.
