Ron Perelman, a consummate dealmaker, has built a fortune on a diverse array of holdings, from candy to cosmetics, and earned a reputation as one of the fiercest corporate raiders of the 1980s. He's a longtime shareholder in Revlon, and owns Humvee-maker AM General, among other firms. He learned about business from his father, attending board meetings with him when he was in elementary school. An avid art collector, in June 2016 he announced a $75 million donation to construct a performing arts center at the World Trade Center site in Manhattan. Perelman has eight children from five wives.
