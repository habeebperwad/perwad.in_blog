Phillip Ragon owns Cambridge, Mass.-based InterSystems, a $574 million (2016 sales) company that sells software enabling hospitals and banks to analyze big data. He founded the company in 1978 and owns all of it. In 2009, following a trip to South Africa where Ragon witnessed the ravages of AIDS, he and his wife Susan (an executive at InterSystems) established the Ragon Institute in partnership with Massachusetts General Hospital, Harvard and MIT to pursue an HIV vaccine.
