Roman Avdeev is the owner of Credit Bank of Moscow, one of Russia's biggest banks by assets. The bank, in which he has a majority stake, went public in June 2015. He bought nearly two-thirds of a pharmacy chain called A5 then in December 2016 he resold it to rival 36.6 in exchange for a 10% stake in the combined company. That same month, he bought a big stake in publicly traded property developer OPIN from billionaire Mikhail Prokhorov; he already owned several residential real estate developments in Moscow. Avdeev has 23 children, 19 of them adopted. He is vegetarian and practices yoga.
