New Brunswick native Arthur Irving owns an estimated two-thirds of Irving Oil, which operates gas stations and oil refineries. Arthur is a third generation member of a Canadian dynasty. His grandfather, James Dergavel Irving, started the family business in the late 1800s. His dad Kenneth Colin (K.C) Irving added oil operations in the 1920s; Arthur and his two brothers reportedly divided up the empire after his death in 1992. Arthur's brother James Irving, also a billionaire, took over a conglomerate that spans shipbuilding to forestry. A third Irving brother, John (known as Jack), headed the family's construction operations and owned part of Irving Oil. He passed away in 2010.
