A Wall Street veteran since 1968, Charles Brandes was drawn to value investing - the search for out of favor companies with the long term fundamentals to recover with a turn in the economy - after meeting Benjamin Graham, the value guru who penned The Intelligent Investor and other books. He founded Brandes Investment Partners in 1974, eventually managing over $100 billion in assets at the firm's 2007 peak. The firm's asset base has shrunk considerably since the financial crisis rocked global equities - as of Dec 2016, Brandes Investment Partners managed $28 billion. Brandes resides in San Diego, California, where his foundation supports the local symphony.
