Koum cofounded Whatsapp, now the world's biggest mobile messaging service, in 2009. Facebook bought the startup for $22 billion in cash and stock in 2014; Koum sits on Facebook's board. Koum immigrated to the U.S. from Ukraine with his mother at age 16 and swept floors to make ends meet. He worked at Yahoo for nearly nine years before leaving to found Whatsapp. In Oct. 2014, Koum donated $555 million of Facebook shares to the Silicon Valley Community Foundation.
