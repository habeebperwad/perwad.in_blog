Oleg Tinkov has a controlling stake in Tinkoff, a leading provider of online financial services in Russia that is based on a high-tech branchless platform. It has issued 6.4 million credit cards and has a nearly 10% market share in Russia. Tinkov got his start selling electronics and white goods in the 1990s, and later sold Tinkoff beer and owned a chain of Tinkoff restaurants. He used the proceeds from the sale of the beer business to go into banking. Goldman Sachs is a minority shareholder. His cycling team Tinkoff-Saxo is being disbanded because of lack of financing.
