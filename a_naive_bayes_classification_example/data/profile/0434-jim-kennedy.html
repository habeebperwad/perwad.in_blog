Jim Kennedy is the grandson of James M. Cox, founder of what today is the $20 billion (sales) automotive and media giant Cox Enterprises. Its businesses include cable TV, radio, newspaper publishing and more. Kennedy is chairman and at one point was CEO, too. He inherited his 25% stake from his mother, Barbara Cox Anthony, who died in 2007, 50 years after James, her father. Cox, which owns Manheim car auctions and Kelley Blue Book, continues to add to the automotive part of its business. In June 2015 it announced a $4 billion acquisition of Dealertrack, a maker of software for car dealerships.
