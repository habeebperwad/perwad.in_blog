Jonathan Nelson is CEO of Providence Equity Partners, the $50 billion (assets under management) private equity firm he founded in 1989. Providence sold the Ironman triathlon series for $900 million in the fall of 2015, quadrupling its initial investment. Nelson studied economics at Brown, then got his MBA at Harvard. Today he is a member of Brown's governing board. In January 2016, he announced a $25 million gift to launch the school's Jonathan M. Nelson Center for Entrepreneurship, which opened in the fall of 2016. He has also served on the board of directors of the Newport, Rhode Island Jazz Festival.
