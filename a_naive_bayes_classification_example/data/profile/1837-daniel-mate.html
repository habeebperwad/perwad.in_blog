Daniel Mate broke into the ranks of the world's billionaires when global commodities trading outfit Glencore went public in 2011. The Spaniard holds a 3% stake in the London-listed company. Mate joined Marc Rich & Co. in 1988 in Switzerland, six years before the firm was bought out by management and rebranded as Glencore. He rose to co-directing the zinc, copper and lead commodity department in 2000 and now runs the zinc trading unit. He's also a longtime director at Peruvian mining company Volcan and owns a number of properties around Madrid.
