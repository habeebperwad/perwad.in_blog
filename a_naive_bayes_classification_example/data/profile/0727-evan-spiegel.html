Snapchat creator Spiegel became the youngest public company CEO when Snap began trading in March 2017. Each day some 150 million people, mostly teens and millennials, use Snapchat to send disappearing messages. Spiegel cofounded the company with Murphy, a Stanford University fraternity brother, in 2011. Spiegel and Murphy each own about 18% of Snap but have voting shares that give them control over the board.
