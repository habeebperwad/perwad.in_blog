Michael Price runs MFP Investors, a New York-based hedge fund that holds stakes in Intel, Hess Corp. and Du Pont. He started his career in finance in 1975 as a research assistant at Heine Securities. By 1988 he was the sole owner of the company. An expert value investor, Price increased the value of the mutual funds he managed to more than $17 billion before selling Heine Securities to Franklin Securities for $670 million in 1996. He donated $25 million to Yeshiva University in 2001 for a biomedical research center in his name and $18 million to his alma mater, the University of Oklahoma, to form the Price College of Business in 1997.
