Lee Sang-Hyuk, the founder of the country's second-biggest unicorn, is a billionaire thanks to his 26% stake in Yello Mobile, an incubator that invests in mobile-based startups. Since its founding in 2012, it's raised $250 million in funding and has a $4 billion valuation. Today Yello's portfolio includes some 60 companies across shopping, media, advertising and travel. Lee worked as an executive for Korean search engine Daum for a year and a half before leaving in 2013.
